Wondering: Number of Siblings — Giovanni (The Turtle)

behind the scenens

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux at Sunday Jun 21st, 2026

When I want to create a fictional story, I often first focus more on world building. I first create the fictional world, and then tell stories within that fictional world.

For me, creating a fictional world involves considering many, many potential ideas for the world building. Some ideas I keep. Some ideas I (eventually) discard.

What If

The premise of this is fan fiction is — what if there was a hidden 5th turtle. What if the TMNT had a lost 5th brother.

And, to get a bit technical, what if this 5th turtle is from the same clutch as the 4 TMNT.

The Author Needs To Know

In world building, even if you don't tell the audience all the details, you (the author) need to know all the details.

So, the TMNT aren't just 4 siblings, but there is a 5th.

But, that makes me wonder — are there more-than 5 siblings‽

Even if the stories we tell only talk about 5 siblings — we need to know if there are more-than 5 siblings. And, if there are more-than 5 siblings — what happened to them.

It is our story, so we can make up whatever number of siblings we want. But, I tend to make things plausible by default.

Turtle Species

I know different (biological) species tend to have different distributions for the number of children the females of the species tend to get pregnant with at the same time.

Although there is some variation — with humans, it tends to be one. But, what about with turtles‽ I assume it probably depends on the species of turtle. Maybe, even other factors, too.

So, what species of turtle did the TMNT mutate from‽

There is actually more-than one "blessed" TMNT fiction. Some TMNT fictions do not specify the species of turtle. Some TMNT fictions do.

The ones that do say it is the red-eared slider turtle. The red-eared slider seems to be a very common pet turtle species.

Red-Eared Slider Clutches

Female red-eared slider turtles seem to most commonly lay about 10 to 15 eggs per clutch. With 5 to 20 eggs per clutch being the usual range.

Although, at the exteme high-end — large, mature female red-eared sliders seem to be able to lay 20+ per clutch, with some even getting up to around 30 per clutch.

And, at the extreme low-end — smaller/younger female red-eared sliders even lay as low a 2 to 8 eggs per clutch.

How Many Siblings

So, how many eggs were in the clutch the TMNT were born from‽

It needs to be at least 5. But, is it exactly 5‽

Do we want to decide this now‽

If we decided it was exactly 5 — it would be plausible, but not typical.

Alternatively, if we decided, for example, there were 13 siblings, that would be in the most common range of 10 to 15 egs per clutch.

Summary

From a biological plausibility point-of-view, we are safe with there being at least 5 turtle siblings in the clutch the TMNT were born from.

But, from a biological plausibility point-of-view, there should probably be 10 to 15 siblings in the clutch.

We could pick the number (of siblings in the clutch) to make the number useful for story telling.

For example, if we wanted to be inspired by various mythologies — some numbers have significance in those myths.

For example, 12 signs in the zodiac, 12 months in many calendars, 12 companions, 12 trials, 12 animals, 12 parts to the cycle, etc.
